Key Findings

DATASET INSIGHTS

Exploratory analysis revealed interesting patterns in how users rate anime and which titles dominate the community.

30.4%
TV Series Dominate
3,402 anime aired on TV — the largest category by count, followed by OVA at 27.7% and Movies at 18.9%.
5.5–8
Rating Sweet Spot
Most anime average ratings cluster between 5.5 and 8.0. User ratings peak around 7–8, skewing left.
−1
Watched But Unrated
Many users watched anime without rating it. These −1 entries were filtered out before training the recommender models.
50+
Rating Threshold
Only users who rated 50 or more anime were included in collaborative filtering — ensuring quality signal over noise.

Methodology

HOW IT
WORKS

Two recommendation approaches power this system — each with distinct strengths.

01
📐
Data Preprocessing
Null values dropped, duplicate ratings removed, special characters cleaned from anime names. Users with fewer than 50 ratings excluded to reduce noise.
02
🤝
Collaborative Filtering
A user-item pivot matrix is built and converted to a sparse CSR matrix. KNN with cosine similarity finds anime with the most similar viewing patterns.
03
🏷️
Content-Based Filtering
Genres are vectorized with TF-IDF. Sigmoid kernel computes pairwise similarity across all anime, enabling recommendations based purely on genre profile.
04
🎯
Top-10 Recommendations
For a given anime title, the system returns the 10 most similar titles ranked by similarity score, with their community ratings displayed.
